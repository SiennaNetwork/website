PRIVACY MEETS DeFi

Sienna is a privacy-first and cross-chain decentralized finance platform where you can privately swap, lend and convert your tokens into their private equivalent.

Private Swaps
SiennaSwap is a privacy-first, cross-chain decentralized exchange where users can swap private tokens in complete privacy.
‍
SiennaSwap runs on Secret Network, which is the first blockchain with privacy-preserving smart-contracts with high scalability and low fees. Transactions are executed almost instantly, and with privacy combined, front-running is effectively mitigated. Welcome to the future of DeFi.
Read more about SiennaSwap
Private Lending
The first private savings protocol on Secret Network. 
Earn stable yield by providing sUST (Secret UST) for others to borrow, or borrow sUST by providing collateral tokens, such as sBTC, sETH, sSCRT, sXMR and sLUNA. 

SiennaLend is the first private lending protocol to enable private lending for multiple blockchain ecosystems while maintaining your privacy. It is permissionless and has no signup requirements, no limit to deposits, no centralized party to freeze your account, and is governed by the SIENNA governance token.

About senius
Senius AG is a software development and design company that focuses on privacy and protecting personal data.
About secret network
Secret Network is a decentralized network of computers (secret nodes) that utilize trusted execution environments (TEEs) to enable secure, private computation over encrypted data. TEEs function like a “black box” for data processing, and they are utilized in all types of everyday platforms such as smartphones and video game consoles.
About Halborn
Halborn was founded in 2019 by renowned ethical hacker Steven Walbroehl and growth hacker Rob Behnke. The fully remote organization has since grown to over 50 of the best and brightest offensive security engineers in the world.
About certik
Certik leads blockchain security by pioneering the use of cutting-edge Formal Verification technology on smart contracts and blockchains. Unlike traditional security audits, Formal Verification mathematically proves program correctness and hacker-resistance.
